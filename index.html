About Me

For over 17 years, I devoted myself to supporting individuals with diverse needs—whether it was helping someone with Cerebral Palsy gain independence or assisting clients on the Autism Spectrum to navigate everyday tasks.

My early experiences in Brazil, followed by roles in Sydney, taught me that compassion, patience, and trust can transform a person's world. I thrived on creating tailored care plans, cooking nourishing meals, and collaborating with families and healthcare professionals to ensure every individual received not just support, but genuine empowerment.

Yet as I continued to grow, I realized there was another way to make a meaningful impact: harnessing the power of technology. That spark led me to enroll in a Software Engineering program with the Institute of Data, where I've discovered a completely new world—one where my empathy and creativity in care work seamlessly blend with problem-solving and coding.

Each day, I'm fascinated by how data-driven tools and intuitive software can improve services for vulnerable populations—making everything from scheduling appointments to accessing community resources faster, easier, and more inclusive.

Today, I carry forward my core values—respect, dignity, and collaboration—into this next chapter of my career. Just as I once tailored individual care plans, I now enjoy designing solutions that solve real problems, streamline processes, and ultimately help more people.

I believe that my background in human-centered care makes me a stronger developer, and I'm eager to see how technology can amplify the impact I've always strived for.Whether in a client's home or behind a keyboard, my mission remains the same: to uplift others, park positive change, and keep learning every step of the way.
